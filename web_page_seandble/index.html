Lucid Dream Control Questionnaire (LDCQ)

About this Questionnaire

A lucid dream is a dream during which you are aware of being in a dream and may involve control of the dream content and events. The Lucid Dream Control Questionnaire (LDCQ) assess you perceived ability to deliberately and successfully control different aspects of your lucid dreams. The questionnaire contains 36 items that cover seven core domains of dream control: own cognition, own emotion, own body, own movement, dream characters, dream environment, and dream physics. Completion takes 10-15 minutes.

How to Respond

For each statement, indicate how often you are typically able to deliberately and successfully achieve the described form of dream control. Use the following scale:
Never = 0; Rarely = 1; Sometimes = 2; Often = 3; Always = 4
If you have never attempted a particular type of dream control, mark it as “N.A.” (Not Applicable).
Please answer the questions based on your typical lucid dreaming experiences rather than a single dream. There are no right or wrong answers, so please respond as honestly as possible.

Important

Your rating should reflect how often you successfully achieve a particular form of dream control when you intentionally attempt it. For example, selecting "Rarely" (score = 1) means that when you attempt this type of dream control, you rarely succeed. It does not mean that you rarely attempt it. Similarly, selecting “N.A.” indicates that you never attempted that form of dream control, it does not imply that you would be unable to perform it.

Calculating and Understanding Your Score

The LDCQ provides two types of scores for each domain:
  • Dream Control Score: The Dream Control Score reflects your perceived ability to successfully control a particular aspect of your lucid dreams. Scores range from 0 to 1 (0 = no perceived control; 1 = complete perceived control). Higher scores indicate greater perceived dream control ability within that domain.
  • Dream Engagement Score: The Dream Engagement Score reflects the breadth of dream control behaviours that you have attempted within a domain. Scores range from 0 to 1 (0 = no engagement; 1 = complete engagement). Higher engagement scores indicated greater breadth and lower scores poorer breadth.

Each item is scored as follows:
Never = 0; Rarely = 1; Sometimes = 2; Often = 3; Always = 4.
Responses marked as “N.A.” are excluded from scoring and are not treated as 0. Selecting “N.A.” indicates that you have not attempted that form of dream control

Calculating the Dream Control Score for each Domain

  1. Add together all item scores within that domain. Exclude any items marked as “N.A.”. This is your total domain score.
  2. Count the number of items you answered within that domain (exclude the “N.A.” responses).
  3. Divide the total domain score by the number of answered items multiplied by 4. The resulting Dream Control Score will range from 0 to 1. Higher scores indicate greater perceived dream control ability within that domain, while lower scores indicate lower perceived control

Example

Suppose you answered four items within a domain with scores of: 3, 2, 4, and “N.A.”.
  1. Your total domains core is: 3 + 2 + 4 = 9 (“N.A.” is excluded from the scoring).
  2. The number of items answered is 3 (“N.A.” is excluded from the count).
  3. The Dream Control Score for that domain is: 9 ÷ (3 x 4) = 9 ÷ 12 = 0.75
This indicates that you successfully achieved 75% of the maximum dream-control score within that domain, suggesting a relatively high level of perceived dream control within that domain.

Calculating the Dream Engagement Score for each Domain

  1. Count the number of items within that domain that were not marked as “N.A.”
  2. Divide this number by the total number of items in that domain.
The resulting Dream Engagement Score will range from 0 to 1. Higher scores indicate that you have attempted a broader range of dream control behaviours within that domain, while lower scores that fewer behaviours have been attempted.

Example

Suppose you answered four items within a domain with scores of: 3, 2, 4, and “N.A.”.
  1. Completed items not marked as “N.A.” = 3
  2. Dream engagement score: 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75
This indicates that you have attempted 75% of the dream control behaviours represented in that domain.
